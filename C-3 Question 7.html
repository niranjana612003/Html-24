Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is a time of joy, family gatherings, and vibrant celebrations. As homes are illuminated with colourful diyas and the night sky sparkles with fireworks, another essential
element adds to the festive spirit: sweets. In India, sweets hold a special place in our hearts and our traditions, especially during Diwali. They symbolize love, prosperity, and the joy of sharing. Each
sweet has its own significance, and together they create a rich tapestry of flavors and emotions that enhance the festive spirit. In the context of Diwali, sweets are often associated with the worship of
Lord Rama. The festival commemorates his return to Ayodhya after defeating the demon king Ravana. People celebrate this victory of good over evil by sharing sweets, as a way to express their
happiness and gratitude. It is a divine offering, symbolizing the abundance of blessings that have come into their lives.
